About

History includes those named in the narratives as well as those silenced in the archive. I aim to bring the latter to the forefront.

My academic training is in history — in the practice of listening to silences, reading against the grain of records, and looking beyond the texts. This discipline shapes everything I make.

In audio, I produce intimate documentary journalism in the tradition of narrative radio: close-mic, slow-burn, character-driven. My current work focuses on South Asian communities; their struggles, their devotion, their experiences.

Photography comes from the same impulse. On camera, as on tape, I work with one subject at a time. A train conductor. A smoker caught mid-thought. A shepherd at altitude. Each image is a document as much as it is a picture.

Work Together

All stories deserve an audience.

I bring archival methods to contemporary journalism — the habit of sitting with a subject long enough to reach the second layer of truth, the patience to return when the first interview wasn't quite enough. I am open to working with publications, cultural institutions, NGOs, and organisations and build something that outlasts news cycles.
